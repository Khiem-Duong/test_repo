Project Overview ▶
FoundersLam — Co-Founder, operations and financial authority  ·  Khiem — Co-Founder, website administration and accounting
HistoryThe founders and their friends have been doing this community support work personally for over a decade. This year they are making it official so that more people and more resources can participate
TypeCommunity initiative. Not a charity. Formally and legally registered in Vietnam as a commercial website
OfficesTwo physical offices — one in Hue, one in Da Nang
ModelSell Hue traditional handicrafts. 50% of net profit (after operating costs) goes directly to community support. 50% goes to product development
Revenue ethicsThe project never asks for money from the public. When people offer donations, this is always declined. Revenue comes only from product sales. This is non-negotiable and must be reflected in all public-facing copy
Geographic focusHue and the surrounding area — because the organisation is still small. As it grows, support may expand to other locations
Primary beneficiariesDisadvantaged people in Hue — primary focus on children's health, living conditions, and education. Also elderly, disabled, and crisis/disaster victims
Beneficiary sourcingThrough school principals, community authority heads, orphanages, hospitals, disadvantaged people centers — plus direct individuals
Beneficiary follow-upQuarterly basis
Consent policyBeneficiaries give explicit consent before being featured on the website. Cases without consent are never published
Success definitionConsistently and sustainably generating income to support disadvantaged people in Hue, especially children
PaymentOnline payment available when products are in stock. When out of stock, an inquiry form replaces the buy button. Payment gateway TBD
CheckoutGuest checkout only. No customer accounts
FinancesOne general bank account, opened exclusively for this purpose, controlled by Lam. Lam has sole authority on spending
Public transparencySimple static total on Impact page showing how much has gone to community support. Monthly and/or quarterly recap posts on the blog showing earnings and where money went. No internal ledger on the site
Annual reportFormal annual impact report planned
CampaignsRun continuously with no funding goals. Three pilot programs already running for years. One is still planned
ToneInformative and formal. Founders stay behind the scenes — no names or photos prominently featured
Homepage feelingProducts are good. Purpose is clear and kind
FuturePossibly register as social enterprise/nonprofit in 3–5 years. Target lifespan of 10–15 years or indefinitely
